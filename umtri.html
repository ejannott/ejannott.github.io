User Trust Study

This is a project that we are currently working on at UMTRI in cooperation with DENSO. Using the 2017 Subaru Legacy that has been supplied to us, our goal is to evaluate how much drivers trust the onboard safety systems. We are specifically looking at how drivers interact with the lane keep assist (LKA) system, as well as the lane departure warning (LDW) system. The overall goal of this study is to figure out a quantifiable way to evaluate user trust in automated driving systems.

LKA is a system that actively tries to keep drivers from unintentionally swaying out of their lane. If the driver is getting too close to the lane lines, the system will activate and the steering wheel will actually pull in the direction it should turn to keep the car centered in the road lane. LKA only activates when the car is going faster than 60 KPH (about 37.2 MPH). LDW is a system that warns the driver they they are departing their lane when they may not intend to. Both of the systems deactivate when the turn signal is turned on, or when the brakes are activated.

Each participant goes on an approximately three hour drive with a researcher in the car. There are 5 cameras in the car that are recording the driver’s movements and reactions, as well as the vehicle systems. We also have a GPS unit rigged up and are recording GPS data from the drives as well.

I personally helped with how we would prepare participants for the drives - methodology of the study and such. Beyond that, I helped prep the camera inside of the Subaru, and ran participants on the drives.
